The first time I volunteered for a Bitcoin conference I met many bitcoiners for the first time. I'm what some people might call socially awkward. We were called nerds back on my day. As a nerd, it's difficult for me to talk to people about Bitcoin. Most of the time people don't even understand the concept I am talking about: Peer-To-Peer electronic cash.

I have been a peer-to-peer network nerd for 25 years. Ironically that makes me feel more comfortable with many of the podcasters and developers in black hoodies than I feel around people wearing suits. The second time I went to a podcast a dude wearing a suit told me he worked on data vending machines. That's more of a "shadowy super coder" ethos than wearing a black hoodie. l still don't get it. Still too socially awkward.

Like [Satoshi says]([https://satoshi.nakamotoinstitute.org/posts/bitcointalk/167/](https://satoshi.nakamotoinstitute.org/posts/bitcointalk/167/)),"**Sorry to be a wet blanket.  Writing a description for this thing for general audiences is bloody hard.  There's nothing to relate it to."**

It's easy to write about Bitcoin to nostr nerds and stacker news enthusiasts, but **it's more difficult to write about our beloved peer-to-peer network and digital signatures to socially adept people**. Socially adept people pay for fancy shoes, fancy pants, and fancy conference tickets. These things signal trust.
